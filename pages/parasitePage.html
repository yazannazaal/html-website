Parasite (2019)

Plot: The story revolves around the Kim family, who are struggling with poverty in Seoul. The son, Ki-woo, secures a tutoring job with the wealthy Park family by posing as a university student. Through a series of schemes, the Kims gradually infiltrate the Parks' household, with each family member assuming roles that seem essential to the Parksâ€™ well-being. As the Kims embed themselves deeper, the tension between the two families escalates, leading to unexpected and dramatic consequences.

Themes: "Parasite" explores themes of class disparity, social mobility, and the complexities of family dynamics. It provides a satirical look at the divide between the wealthy and the poor, highlighting the lengths to which individuals will go to improve their circumstances.

Reception: The film was a critical and commercial success, winning the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival. At the 92nd Academy Awards, it won Best Picture, Best Director, Best Original Screenplay, and Best International Feature Film, making history in several ways. "Parasite" was praised for its inventive storytelling, strong performances, and its ability to engage audiences across different cultures.

Impact: "Parasite" has been hailed as a landmark film for its bold storytelling and its role in breaking down barriers in global cinema. It sparked conversations about class issues and remains influential for its innovative approach to genre and narrative.
